    Newborn

    Newborn babies exhibit a wide range of behaviors that are essential for their development and survival. Here are some common newborn behaviors:

    1 Month Old

    A one-month-old baby is becoming more alert and responsive. They are also adapting to their surroundings and schedule

    2 Months Old

    A 2-month-old baby is curious, social, and active. They are developing their motor skills and learning to interact with their environment

    3 Months Old

    A 3-month old baby will typically display behaviors like smiling at familiar faces, following moving objects with their eyes, starting to coo and gurgle, lifting their head during tummy time, reaching for objects with their hands, recognizing your voice, and turning towards sounds, showing a growing awareness of their surroundings and developing social interaction skills

    4 Months Old

    At four months, babies are becoming more active and social, and are developing their communication skills. They're also learning to interact with the world around them

    5 Months Old

    A 5-month old baby typically exhibits behaviors like rolling over, reaching for objects, grabbing toys, babbling with different sounds, showing more distinct emotions through facial expressions, sitting with support, improved head control, exploring their environment by putting things in their mouth, and potentially displaying some fear of strangers, all while becoming more interactive and engaged with people around them; they are also learning about cause and effect through play.

    6+ Months Old

    A 6-month old baby typically exhibits behaviors like babbling with sounds like "ma" and "da", reaching for objects, recognizing familiar faces, showing interest in mirrors, sitting with support, starting to show emotions through vocalizations, and may even begin to show some anxiety around strangers; they are also becoming more mobile, potentially starting to roll over and push up on their arms to prepare for crawling.

    Behavior:

    • Sit up with some support
    • Roll over
    • Rock back and forth on hands and knees
    • Push up with straight arms during tummy time
    • Transfer objects from one hand to the other
    • Pick up a dropped object
    12+ Months Old

    A 12-month-old baby may exhibit a variety of behaviors, including social, emotional, cognitive, and physical skills.

    1 Year Old

    A 1-year-old's behavior typically includes increased mobility, attempts at independence, exploring their environment through touch and taste, showing strong attachments to caregivers, potential separation anxiety, expressing needs through gestures and basic words like "mama" and "dada," enjoying interactive games like peek-a-boo, and may exhibit temper tantrums when frustrated, as they are learning to communicate their desires and are still developing self-regulation skills

    2 Years Old

    A typical 2-year-old's behavior often includes frequent tantrums, strong emotions, defiant behavior, imitative play, increased independence, potential hitting or biting, mood swings, separation anxiety, and a growing ability to express themselves through language, even if sometimes with mispronounced words; essentially, they are exploring their world with a lot of energy and may sometimes struggle to control their emotions due to their rapid development
